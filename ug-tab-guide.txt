3. Chords formatting rules
3.1. Formatting

A. Chords lining  REQUIRED
Chords should be specified above the lines of a text as they are to be played. Use N.C. if nothing is played on that line. When one chord with no changes appears more than once on successive lines - you can skip it. However, to prevent a big amount of a text without chords (for example, rap text), you need to add repeated chords at least on every fourth line.

Am            C                      Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G        G7            C            Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Am            C                      Dm
When our friends talk about you all it does is just tear me
G        G7            C            Em/B
Cause my heart breaks a little when I hear your name
Correct
Chords are specified across entire lyrics

Am               C                   Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G        G7            C            Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
 
When our friends talk about you all it does is just tear me
 
Cause my heart breaks a little when I hear your name
Incorrect
Chords are missing in full or in part

B. Timing  REQUIRED
Place the chords according to the time they are to be played.

Am               C                   Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G               G7            C               Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Correct
Chords are placed according to the time they are to be played

Am C Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G G7 C Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Incorrect
Chords are not placed according to the time they are to be played

C. Chords position  REQUIRED
Chords are located ABOVE the text.

Am               C                   Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G               G7            C               Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Correct
Chords are placed above the text

[Am]Same bed, but [C]it feels just alittle [Dm]bit bigger now
[G]Our song on the [G7]radio, but it [C]don't sound [Em/B]the same

                    
Incorrect
Chords are placed inside the text

D. Extra Empty Lines  RECOMMENDED
Don't leave empty lines between lyrics and chords. This will make the tab more readable and accurate and help people scroll or print your tab more easily.


Correct
Chords are located right above the words


                    
Incorrect
Extra empty lines between lyrics and chords.

3.3. Other

A. Letter case  REQUIRED
The first letter of a chord is capitalized, the rest are in lower case: Am, Dsus4 etc.

Am               C                   Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G               G7            C               Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Correct
All chords start with a capital letter

am               c                   dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
g               g7            c               em/b
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
Incorrect
Chords begin with a lowercase letter

B. Extra symbols  RECOMMENDED
Do not add extra symbols or text in chord lines. If chord is to be played with any particular technique, add the description technique into the legend.

Am               C                   Dm
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
G               G7            C               Em/B
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same
************************************

| *  strum once

************************************
Correct
There are no unnecessary symbols in the chord lines, just the chords

(Am*)---        (C*)---              (Dm*)
Same bed, but it feels just a little bit bigger now
(G)-----------(G7)----------------(C)-----------(Em/B)
Our song on the radio, but it don't sound the same




                    
Incorrect
The legend for the used symbols is missing

C. "No chord"  RECOMMENDED
N.C. notation is used the same way as a regular chord.

[Verse 1]

N.C.                                    A C F
Other days I would just rather be alone
N.C.                                                  A C F
Some days I feel so insignificant like nothing even matters
F           N.C.
I’ve been caught under the radar so help me please
Correct
When you need to draw attention to the fact that there shouldn't be played anything at some particular moment, use the abbreviation "N.C."

D. Fingering  RECOMMENDED
If you want to specify a special chord variation, specify their fingerings in the beginning of the tab, by using the following template "chord name – fret number", and starting from the thickest string.

Chords:
Am 577555
Em7 x79787
Cmaj7 x-x-10-12-12-12
Correct
Chord names are specified, all the fingerings are present

F. Chord names  RECOMMENDED
Use only common chord names and symbols. Otherwise, our algorithm will not recognize them, and users will not be able to see chord shapes in your tab. We don't accept any additional chord names.
